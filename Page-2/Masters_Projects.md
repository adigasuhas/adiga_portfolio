# 5] Superconductor Augmented World

- Machine learning studies on superconductors have largely relied on composition-based descriptors due to the limited availability of structured experimental datasets.

- Despite significant progress in materials informatics, there remains no unified superconducting materials database containing experimental properties, theoretical descriptors, and crystal structure information.

- In this project, we developed a large-scale text-mining pipeline to extract more than 35 material properties from over 4000 published scientific articles.

- The resulting dataset is designed to support improved machine learning modelling, structure–property analysis, and data-driven superconductor discovery.

- The project combines natural language processing, scientific document understanding, and materials informatics workflows.

# 4] Study of Low Lattice Thermal Conductivity in Doped GeTe using Universal Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials

- GeTe is a prominent thermoelectric material with experimentally reported \(zT\) values reaching up to 2.6 upon doping.

- Understanding the microscopic origin of low lattice thermal conductivity is important for designing high-performance thermoelectric materials.

- Introducing dopants significantly increases the computational complexity of first-principles calculations, particularly for phonon and vibrational studies where symmetry is strongly reduced.

- In this work, we use Universal Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials (UMLIPs) and fine-tuning strategies to study the chemistry and vibrational physics of doped GeTe systems at a fraction of the computational cost of DFT calculations.

- The project focuses on scalable atomistic simulations for complex disordered thermoelectric materials.

# 3] Vibrational and Raman Study in B-type Carbonated Hydroxyapatite

- B-type carbonated hydroxyapatite (B-CHap) is a biologically relevant material commonly found in teeth, bones, and fossilized biominerals.

- Variations in sample coloration can potentially indicate radioactive contamination or environmental exposure.

- In this study, Raman vibrational modes were investigated using first-principles calculations to support and interpret experimental measurements.

- The work focused on identifying characteristic vibrational signatures and understanding structure–spectroscopy relationships in carbonated hydroxyapatite systems.

# 2] Accelerating Search for Superconductors using Machine Learning

- The discovery of new superconductors has traditionally relied heavily on experimental trial-and-error approaches.

- While first-principles methods such as Density Functional Theory (DFT) are effective for conventional superconductors, accurately predicting the critical temperature of unconventional systems remains challenging.

- In this work, we developed machine learning models to predict superconducting critical temperatures using experimentally reported data.

- Quantum Structure Diagram-based descriptors were used to train and interpret the models.

- The project focused on combining physically meaningful descriptors with data-driven approaches for accelerated superconducting materials discovery.
