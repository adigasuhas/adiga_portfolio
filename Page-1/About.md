# About

I am Suhas Adiga, a final-year M.S. (Research) student at the Jawaharlal Nehru Centre for Advanced Scientific Research (JNCASR), Bengaluru, where I work under the supervision of [Prof. Umesh V. Waghmare](https://scholar.google.co.in/citations?user=EY7WIIwAAAAJ&hl=en). My research lies at the intersection of materials science and machine learning, with a particular emphasis on data-driven strategies for the discovery and design of functional materials. I completed my Bachelor’s degree in Physical Science from the University of Delhi.

Beginning in October 2026, I will join [Prof. Matthew J. Rosseinsky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matthew_Rosseinsky), [Dr. George R. Darling](https://openalex.org/authors/A5080860663), and [Dr. Chris Collins]([https://openalex.org/authors/A5080860663](https://www.liverpool.ac.uk/people/chris-michael-collins)) at the Materials Innovation Factory, University of Liverpool, as part of Cohort 3 of the [EPSRC CDT in Digital and Automated Materials Chemistry](https://www.liverpool.ac.uk/doctoral-training/digital-and-automated-materials-chemistry/). 

My broader scientific interests span materials informatics, high-throughput computational workflows, and the development of machine learning and deep learning methodologies for accelerated materials discovery. I am especially interested in building computational frameworks that integrate physical understanding, statistical learning, and scalable automation to address complex problems in condensed matter physics and materials chemistry.


I strongly believe that scientific progress benefits from openness, reproducibility, and collaborative exchange. As such, I value open-source scientific ecosystems and view accessible research infrastructure as essential for accelerating innovation and broadening participation in science.

Beyond lab, I enjoy playing badminton, and do plane spotting. While doing science always was my primary interest, I have dreamed to be a loco-pilot, pilot and and now, rather whimsically, a coffee-grower.
