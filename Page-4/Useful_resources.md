# Useful Resources

## Python for Materials Science
[Logo.jpg]
A GitHub repository containing tutorials on Python utilities relevant to Materials Science, covering topics from basic concepts to advanced libraries in the field. The content is presented in the way I have learned and applied it throughout my work. The repository is expected to be completed within 10 weeks. Feedback and suggestions for improvement are welcome.

Access the tutorials on the **[Python for Materials Science GitHub Repository](https://github.com/adigasuhas/Python-for-Materials-Science)**.

## Machine Learning

### Foundations of Machine Learning

- **[Stanford CS 229: Machine Learning](https://youtube.com/playlist?list=PLoROMvodv4rMiGQp3WXShtMGgzqpfVfbU)** – Comprehensive lecture series taught by Prof. Andrew Ng.
- **[University of Waterloo SYDE 522: Machine Intelligence](https://youtube.com/playlist?list=PLpFYkKZ3fmP7T0datl4ghRebc7nlkmgVI)** – Winter 2019 curriculum taught by Professor H.R. Tizhoosh.

### Machine Learning in Materials Science

- **[Imperial College London MATE70026: Machine Learning For Materials](https://aronwalsh.github.io/MLforMaterials/Overview.html)** – Specialized academic course taught by Prof. Aron Walsh.
- **[Machine Learning in Materials Science (Reference Book)](https://www.google.co.in/books/edition/Machine_Learning_in_Materials_Science/F6R1EAAAQBAJ?hl=en&gbpv=0)** – Comprehensive textbook by F. Oviedo, K. T. Butler, and P. Canepa (Wiley-VCH, 2024).
- **[Introduction to Materials Informatics](https://enze-chen.github.io/mi-book-2021/intro.html)** – Digital interactive book by Prof. Enze Chen and Prof. Mark Asta.
- **[Materials Informatics Lecture Series](https://youtube.com/playlist?list=PLL0SWcFqypCl4lrzk1dMWwTUrzQZFt7y0)** – Video playlist taught by Prof. Taylor Sparks.

## Scientific Slide Design

- **[Make Attractive Scientific Figures in PowerPoint](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MazT8kT43PQ&t)** – Video tutorial on color, typography, and element positioning by Dr. Anubhav Jain (Atom to Materials channel).
- **[Ram Seshadri’s Slide Graphics Thread](https://mrlweb.mrl.ucsb.edu/~seshadri/GraphicsThread-May2020.pdf)** – Outstanding, detailed guidelines on preparing clear, high-quality figures and graphics for presentations and publications.
- **[Coolors Palette Generator](https://coolors.co/generate)** – A powerful, interactive utility for generating clean, harmonious color palettes and gradients.
- **[WebAIM Color Contrast Checker](https://webaim.org/resources/contrastchecker/)** – Essential tool for verifying text-to-background contrast ratios against web accessibility standards.
- **[Making Scientific Figures with Illustrator and Blender](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3IuoK07YDds&list=PLRCLlYmhDNMyTLhQCtlPryR_6odf6tBBF)** – Comprehensive tutorial series on scientific illustration and 3D modeling by Christopher Gutierrez (UCLA).

*Note: The slide design resources were introduced to me by my senior, **[Dr. Arijit Sinha](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=0Rh-oVQAAAAJ&hl=en)**; I am deeply grateful for his excellent guidance.*
